Chapter #1: About Me

Divided between Romanticism and Contemporaneity

Born in Sintra, this beautiful village, on the westernmost tip of Europe, would end up shaping the person I am today.

The romantic ideals of residential and landscape architecture practiced in the 19th and early 20th centuries ended up being primarily responsible for my sensibility and aesthetics. These places were designed with great care and intention, in order to impress, to transport anyone who experiences them to another reality, to emphasize emotions and the connection with nature.

By intuition, I drew inspiration from this, both in physical and digital media.

Surprise and the unexpected are the reactions I wish to convey with what I produce, regardless of the medium used.

Wolf Saint Lake

Wolf Saint Lake was born out of the need to use my name as a branding image.

"Wolf" is the meaning of my surname Lopes, and "Saint Lake" is the original meaning of Tiago.

"Of the Moon Mountain" represents Sintra, having been the name used by the Romans when referring to this magical place.

Digital media has always fascinated me and I believe I grew up at the perfect time when its mass dissemination started to take place.

Video games have always had a special place in my life, and were the bridge for design, when I worked in some of the most visited websites in Portugal, such as Eurogamer.

Although I started with digital design, product design and packaging have always fascinated me.

Self-teaching allowed me to learn the bases of design principles, culminating, after some freelance work, in me joining the design team of Técnico Lisboa.

The last few years have allowed me to do a lot of experimentation in different areas, which would culminate in the secret project that involves them all.

Design, without bullshit

When I started to work professionally in Design, a lot was happening. Preprocessors and frameworks emerged like mushrooms. It seemed that everyone had the answer to the industry's problems, whatever they were.

Illustrious designers, who work with the largest companies in the world, began to boast at conferences, with admission fees paid in gold by hopefuls, trying to gain the knowledge of the gurus.

Over the years I have lost patience for the entire bullshit of the industry.

The most valuable lesson I learned was to focus on what is intended. Do not do for the sake of doing, do not use it for the sake of using it. Realizing whether something makes sense. Everything imposed on us limits our imagination and process of creation.

My areas of interest within design touch several points, from web design to editorial. I like to have ideas and to experiment. To see if I like something, I test it and if I don’t, I start again.

This makes me constantly interested in other areas and, for that reason, it is impossible for me to identify myself within a specific category.

Chapter #3

The Secret Project

A game of mystery and deceptions.

This is a project that was born from a combination of several passions. Romantic ideals, writing, and design. It all comes together in a story that tells the adventures of Artur, a detective cursed with what, for many people, is a gift.

Artur Bartolomeu tries to unravel the murderer of Hector Moore, a famous English archaeologist, at Lisbon’s Saint Carlos theater.

But when he receives a mysterious order a few days later, he realizes that there is much more to it than the obvious.

Artur travels through England and meanwhile, discovers the coverup for something big, in an adventure that will take the players through a journey to discover the secrets of the beginning of civilization.
